I am a research scientist with a strong background in the chemical and biological sciences. I enjoy working across disciplines and connecting research fields. I am a long-time advocate of integrating advanced statistics, structural bioinformatics, and machine learning (ML) algorithms into our drug discovery strategies. Over the last 16 years, I have seamlessly integrated computational techniques into natural product drug discovery and peptide drug design campaigns. I contributed to multiple research collaborations involving multiple public-private partnerships in interdisciplinary environments across the globe: French National Scientific Research Centre (CNRS) teaming with Laboratoires Pierre Fabre (France), Institute of Molecular Bioscience at the University of Queensland (Australia) in collaboration with biotech subsidiary Noscira (Spain), with pharma giant Pfizer (USA), and the Pharmacy Australian Centre of Excellence PACE at the University of Queensland alongside biotech company Protagonist (Australia). Our strategies were applied to biologically relevant protein targets (kinases, GPCRs, transcription factors) in application to cancer treatments, type 2 diabetes, neurodegenerative disorders, neglected diseases (malaria, leishmania), and antibiotic resistance.

 

In the last six years, I have led an independent research laboratory at the Centre for Research and Advanced Studies of the National Polytechnic Institute (CINVESTAV-IPN) in Mexico. My research interests lie at the crossroads between protein engineering, drug discovery, and artificial intelligence, helping the development of peptide therapeutics. Together with my students, I have developed statistical pipelines, machine-learning models, and protein structure predictions, leveraging algorithms like ProteinMPNN and tools like AlphaFold2, to support our peptide design pipeline. We have also tackled limitations linked to biological experimental processes, such as representation learning, data scarcity, and model biases.

 

I am now exploring new opportunities in the public or private sectors, tackling challenges in drug discovery, health, energy, or food security by leveraging protein engineering and artificial intelligence technologies.
