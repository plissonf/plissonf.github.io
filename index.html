I am a research scientist with a strong background in the chemical and biological sciences. I enjoy working across disciplines and connecting research fields. Over the last 16 years, I have mastered multistep organic synthesis, analytical techniques such as mass spectrometry (EI, ESI, MALDI-TOF), spectroscopy (IR, UV, 1/2D-NMR, CD), liquid chromatography (Silica gel, TLC, HPLC, UPLC), manual and automated solid-phase peptide synthesis. In addition, I have seamlessly integrated advanced statistics, structural bioinformatics, and artificial intelligence in natural product drug discovery and peptide drug design campaigns. I contributed to 5 research collaborations involving multiple public-private partnerships. Our strategies were applied to biologically relevant protein targets (kinases, GPCRs, transcription factors) and phenotypic screenings in application to cancer treatments, type 2 diabetes, neurodegenerative disorders, neglected diseases (malaria, leishmania), and antibiotic resistance.

In the last six years, I have led an independent research laboratory at the Centre for Research and Advanced Studies of the National Polytechnic Institute (CINVESTAV-IPN) in Mexico. My research interests lie at the crossroads between protein engineering, drug discovery, and artificial intelligence, helping the development of peptide therapeutics. Together with my students, I have developed statistical pipelines, ML models, and protein structure predictions to support our peptide design pipeline. We have also tackled limitations linked to biological experimental processes, such as representation learning, data scarcity, and model biases.

I am now exploring new academic and industrial challenges across different sectors (health, agriculture, energy) that capitalize on in-depth statistical analysis, artificial intelligence, and/or protein engineering. Don't hesitate to contact me.
